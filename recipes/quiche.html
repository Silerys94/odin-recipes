Bacon & Swiss Quiche

This Bacon and Swiss Cheese Quiche brings back warm memories of dinners at home with Mom. Even though it’s technically an egg pie, I never thought of it as just breakfast—Mom always made it for dinner, and somehow it felt perfect any time of day. Rich with smoky bacon and creamy Swiss cheese, this quiche is a comforting, savory slice of home that’s just as good for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • 1/4 cup neutral oil (like vegetable or canola)

For the filling:

  • 3 beaten eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 1/4 cup sliced green onions
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon pepper
  • Dash of ground nutmeg
  • 3/4 cup chopped cooked bacon
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Swiss cheese (6 oz)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F.
  2. Combine flour, oil, and water in a bowl. Mix until a dough forms. Press into a pie pan to form the crust. No need to pre-bake.
  3. Whisk together eggs, milk, green onion, salt, pepper, and nutmeg in a large bowl.
  4. Stir in chopped cooked bacon.
  5. Add cheese to the egg mixture and stir well.
  6. Pour filling into the prepared pie crust.
  7. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until a knife inserted near the center comes out clean. Cover crust edges with foil if they brown too quickly.
  8. Let stand for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

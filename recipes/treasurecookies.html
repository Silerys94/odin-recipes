Chocolate Chip Treasure Cookies

These Chocolate Chip Treasure Cookies will always taste like Christmas to me. For years, my mom swore they were our top-secret family recipe—something magical passed down and never to be shared. She guarded it like gold, baking them every holiday with a wink and a "you'll" get the recipe when you're older.”

Years later, living far from home in Indiana and craving that nostalgic taste, I finally asked her for it. She (reluctantly) sent it to me, and I headed to the store to grab the ingredients. That's when I saw it—right there on the label of the Eagle Brand sweetened condensed milk can—our "secret" family recipe in bold print for the world to see!

Still, even with its secret out, these cookies remain a treasured tradition. Buttery, chewy, chocolatey, and rich with coconut and graham cracker crumbs, they're a bite of holiday magic—just like mom used to make (and pretend she invented).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/2 cup all purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 (14 oz.) can Eagle Brand® Sweetened Condensed Milk
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 1 1/3 cup flaked coconut
  • 2 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts

Instructions

  1. HEAT oven to 375ºF. Combine graham cracker crumbs, flour and baking powder in small bowl.
  2. BEAT sweetened condensed milk and butter in large bowl until smooth.
  3. ADD crumb mixture; mix well. Stir in coconut, chocolate chips and walnuts. Drop by rounded tablespoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheets.
  4. BAKE 8 to 10 minutes or until lightly browned. Cool.
